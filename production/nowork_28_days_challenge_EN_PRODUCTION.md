## DAY 0 – This is not an AI challenge. This is an upgrade to how you work.

> **📧 Subject:** It starts today. Here is how the challenge works.
> **👁 Preheader:** 28 days, 5–7 minutes a day, one real shift in how you work.

AI is everywhere right now. New tools, new features, new promises. And that is exactly why it is easy to end up just trying more and more things without really changing how you work.

This challenge was created to help you do the opposite.

The goal is not to learn as many AI tools as possible. The goal is to start using AI practically, regularly, and in a way that genuinely makes your work easier.

Each day should take about 5–7 minutes. Each day, you should take away something useful:
- a new perspective on your work,
- one small practical step,
- a concrete output,
- or a simple improvement to something you already do.

Step by step, you are not just "playing with AI." You are building:
- better habits,
- less friction in your work,
- your own prompts, templates, and workflows,
- and most importantly, a new way of thinking about what really has to be done manually.

And one more important thing: if you come across something you already know during this challenge, that is completely fine. You can go back to an earlier challenge, expand something you already use, improve an existing prompt or workflow, or simply spend five minutes deliberately practicing something you want to use more often in real life.

The point of this challenge is not to complete everything. The point is to move a little further every day.

## DAY 1 – You are not paid for work. You are paid for the outcome.

> **📧 Subject:** You are not paid for work. You are paid for outcomes.
> **👁 Preheader:** One shift in thinking that changes everything else.

When work piles up, most people automatically focus on one thing: how to get everything done.

But this is exactly where the difference between normal work mode and an AI-first approach starts to show.

The point is not just to do things faster. First, you need to pause and ask yourself what outcome you are actually trying to achieve.

As soon as you start thinking more about the outcome than about the task itself, it becomes much easier to see what can be done more simply, can be delegated to AI, does not have to be created from scratch, or maybe does not need to be done at all.

## DAY 4 – A prompt is not a question. It is a work brief.

> **📧 Subject:** Your prompts are questions. They should be briefs.
> **👁 Preheader:** Same principle as delegating to a person. The clearer the brief, the better the result.

Many people think being good at AI means knowing secret formulas or complicated prompt tricks. In reality, one much simpler skill matters more: knowing how to brief work well.

- goal,
